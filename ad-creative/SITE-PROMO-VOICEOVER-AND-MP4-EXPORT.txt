SECTION A — FULL VOICEOVER (ENGLISH) — ~ 90 to 100 SECONDS
================================================================================
Read slowly. Breathe at each " / "

---

Hello. I want to show you a website called “Life Decision Engine,” at
  lifedecisions dot space. /

It was built for one job: to help you think through BIG life decisions in a
  structured way. Not with endless generic chat — but with clear building
  blocks you can actually use. /

So what is on the site? /

The heart of it is the analyzer. You write your main question: for example
  a career move, a relocation, a relationship, or a money fork. /

You can add context and your limits. Then the engine returns a real report: /
  several scenarios — a better path, a rough path, a likely path. /
  It also looks at your case through a few “lenses,” like money, how you
  feel, risks, and opportunity. /

You get a time view — a few months ahead, a couple of years, and longer. /
  And a score, with a written reason — to give you a compass, not a
  superstition. /

On the same project you will find: a public community space for light
  questions, a blog and playbooks with longer writing, a directory where you
  can look for a psychologist, legal, or money professional, languages for
  many countries, and simple explainers for privacy and how the tool uses
  A.I. /

It is important to say: this is decision support, not a substitute for
  emergency care, or your doctor, or your lawyer. /

The core experience is on the open web, so you can try the flow without
  signing up to another social network. /

If a major choice is in front of you, go to lifedecisions dot space, run the
  analyzer once, and read the report with a calmer week in mind. /

---
Word count: about 260 words. At ~140 words per minute (clear speech), the
  full block is roughly 1 minute 50 seconds. If you need exactly one minute,
  use SECTION B (short) or cut the “community + blog + directory” sentence.

================================================================================
SECTION D — HOW TO GET A DOWNLOADABLE MP4 (STEP BY STEP)
================================================================================

(1) PREP
  - Copy the voiceover you want from Section A or B into a text file or
    teleprompter app.
  - Open https://lifedecisions.space in a browser to record, or use images
    from: ad-creative/WEBSITE-AD-VIDEO-PRODUCTION.txt

(2) ON WINDOWS — BUILT-IN (simple)
  - Press Windows key + G → Xbox Game Bar → Capture. Record a tab with the
    site, or your slides.
  - Video saves under Videos\Captures\ as .mp4. That file can be moved,
    posted, or re-uploaded anywhere.

(3) ON PHONE — CapCut (very common)
  - Install CapCut. New project → add photos or screen recording → add audio
    (record voice, or TTS) → place text captions if you want.
  - Top right: Export → 1080p → Save to gallery. The result is an MP4 on the
    phone. From Gallery you can Share to Instagram, YouTube, Telegram, Drive.

(4) IN THE BROWSER — Canva
  - canva.com → Video → use template “promo” or “product”.
  - Upload logo from your repo: public/logo-192.png
  - Paste your voice with Canva’s record, or import an MP3 you made elsewhere.
  - Share → Download → MP4.

(5) TTS (text-to-speech) for the English script
  - Read aloud in Edge browser (select text → right-click → Read aloud) and
    record the speaker output with (2), OR use any TTS you trust; keep
    speed around ninety percent of default so it stays clear.

(6) “DOWNLOAD” FOR SHARING
  - The MP4 on your device IS the file you “download” in the sense of keeping
    a local copy. To share without losing quality: upload the same file to
    Google Drive or Dropbox → get a link → or upload to YouTube (Unlisted) so
    you can share a link. Social apps also accept the MP4 directly from your
    gallery.

(7) LEGAL / IMAGE RIGHTS
  - Site screenshots: yours.
  - Unsplash photos: see Unsplash license; credit photographers when the
    platform requires it. For paid ads, follow the ad network’s rules.
